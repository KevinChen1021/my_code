In Python, "pickling" is the process of converting an object into 
a byte stream, which allows you to store the data on your disk 
and reload it later in its exact original state.
Why use to_pickle instead of to_csv?
While CSV files are common, to_pickle offers several advantages for data analysis:
•Preserves Data Types: If you have specific column types (like datetime, category, or int64), a pickle file 
remembers them. When you reload a CSV , Pandas often has to "guess" the data types again.
•Speed: Pickling is generally much faster for both reading and writing compared to text-based formats like 
CSV or Excel.
•Compression Support: It easily supports compression (like .gzip or .zip) to save disk space.

Concept of an effective rate 
The rate in pv(), fv(), pmt(), nper() functions are all 
effective period rate. 
To estimate the future value of one given present 
value,     we have the following formula
FV = PV *(1+R)^n
FV is the future value      PV is the present value 
R  is an effective rate      n  is the number of periods
R and n should be consistent:
if means that R is the effective annual rate then n is 
the number of years

--- PAGE 40 ---
APR and a compounding frequency
• APR stands for the Annual Percentage Rate. 
• Note that APR is not an effective rate except when compounded annually . 
• Assume we deposit $100. 
a) APR is 10% compounded annually => effective annual rate is 10%. 
[we will receive $10 at the end of the year]
b) APR is 10% compounded semi-annually => effective semi-annual rate is 5%. 
[We will receive $5 every half-year and $10 in one year.]
c) APR is 10% compounded quarterly => effective quarterly rate is 2.5. 
[For each quarter, we will receive $2.5, and $10 in one year .]

What is a 'Zero-Coupon Bond’?
A zero-coupon bond, also known as an "accrual bond, "    is a debt 
security that doesn't pay interest (a coupon)    but is traded at a 
deep discount, rendering profit at maturity when the bond is 
redeemed for its full face value.
Some zero-coupon bonds are issued as such, while others  are 
bonds that have been stripped of their coupons by a  financial 
institution and then repackaged as zero-coupon bonds. Because 
they offer the entire payment at maturity,   zero-coupon bonds tend 
to fluctuate in price much more than coupon bonds.
• http://www.investopedia.com/terms/z/zero-couponbond.asp

YTM: yield to maturity
Yield to maturity (YTM) is the total return anticipated on a 
bond if the bond is held until the end of its lifetime. 
Yield to maturity is considered a long-term bond yield, 
but is expressed as an annual rate. In other words, it is 
the internal rate of return of an investment in a bond if 
the investor holds the bond until maturity and if all payments 
are made as scheduled.
http://www.investopedia.com/terms/y/yieldtomaturity.asp

Duration is a measure of the sensitivity of the price -- the value of

rates. Duration is expressed as a number of years. Bond prices are

--- PAGE 83 ---
def Duration (year,fv,rate,couponRate,freq):
import numpy_financial as npf
n=year*freq
coupon=fv*couponRate/freq
B=bondPrice(year,fv,rate,couponRate,freq)
D=0 
for i in range(1,n+1):
t=i/freq
w=npf.pv(rate/freq,i,0,coupon)/B
D+=w*t
if i==n:
w=npf.pv(rate/freq,i,0,fv)/B
D+=w*t
return round(D,2),round(B,2)

Mean reversion is the financial theory that asset prices, returns, and volatility tend to return to 
their long-term historical average over time. This concept acts as a contrarian trading strategy, 
aiming to profit from extreme market moves—buying low when prices fall below average and 
selling high when they rise above—assuming temporary imbalances will correct.
